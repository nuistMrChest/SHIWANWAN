关于艺术家 | About

Shi Wanwan is an artist, writer and curator who specializes in site-specific creations. Currently, he is teaching at the School of Art of Soochow University and serves as a master's supervisor. He obtained a Master of Fine Arts degree from the Sculpture Department of the Central Academy of Fine Arts and a Doctor of Arts degree from the School of Intermedia Art of the China Academy of Art.

As a writer and researcher, Shi Wanwan has been constantly focusing on the relationship between site-specific art and the era. His recent monograph, "The Lost Site: The Mechanism Critique and Transcendence of Chinese Site-Specific Art" (Hubei Fine Arts Publishing House, 2023), offers an in-depth exploration of the subject. The papers he has recently published include "Neither Dogmatic nor Compromising" (Art Monthly, 2023), "Reflections on Interventional Art" (New Arts, 2022), and "The Critique of Space Production: The Establishment of a New Type of Public Art" (Public Art, 2019).

In addition to academic achievements, he has actively engaged in educational outreach. Shi Wanwan has delivered specialized lectures at esteemed institutions including the Sculpture Department of the Central Academy of Fine Arts, the School of Art and the School of Media and Communication of Soochow University, the School of Fine Arts of Nanjing Normal University, the School of Media and Communication of Nanjing University of the Arts, and the Suzhou Bay Digital Art Museum.

As an artist, Shi Wanwan’s solo exhibitions and individual projects have been held at the Suzhou Jinji Lake Art Museum (2023), the Ox Warehouse in Macau (2020), the Open Contemporary Art Center in Taipei (2019), the Art Museum of Nanjing University of the Arts (2017), the A4 Art Museum in Chengdu (2013), and the Tang Contemporary Art Center (2010). Shi Wanwan's works have also been widely exhibited in international art institutions including the Taiwan Contemporary Culture Lab of the Air Force General Headquarters (2021), the UCCA Dune Art Museum (2020), the Guan Shanyue Art Museum in Shenzhen (2019), the Taikang Space (2013), the Ullens Center for Contemporary Art (2013), the Special Theme Exhibition of the Asian Video Tokyo Art Fair (2013), the Long March Space (2005), and the Shanghai Duolun Museum of Modern Art (2004).

Shi Wanwan’s curatorial projects have been presented at the "Xukou River Estuary" On-site Creation Exhibition of the Xukou 21 Public Art Project in Suzhou (2023), the "Day and Night" Young Artists Exhibition, the "Dream Without Borders" The 3rd International Public Art Invitational Exhibition of the Hangzhou Bay New Area in Ningbo (2021), the "Cell Project": The Public Art Exhibition in Xinhua Community during the Shanghai Urban Space Art Season in Shanghai (2021), and the "Second Site", PPPP Space in Beijing(2019) and other contemporary art exhibitions.Works have been recognized and collected by prominent art institutions and individuals, including the Hunan Art Museum, the Art Museum of Nanjing University of the Arts, the Art Museum of the Central Academy of Fine Arts, the Tang Contemporary Art Center, and the Shanghai Oil Tank Art Center. Shi Wanwan was also honored with the Best Creativity Award at the 2019 Suzhou International Design Week and was nominated for the 8th Tomorrow Sculpture Award.
